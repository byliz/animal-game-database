What is Kemono and Kemonomimi?

Kemono is the Japanese word for "animal" or "beast" and refers to anthropomorphic animal characters - animal characters with human characteristics such as talking and walking on two legs. Kemonomimi means "animal ears" and refers to zoomorphic human characters - human characters with animal traits such as animal ears and tails. Kemono differ from western anthropomorphic characters in that they usually follow the "cute" and "cool" trends of anime or manga art styles including large eyes and short snouts, giving them a stylized form that is closer to human than animal. Because anthropomorphism is a spectrum, animal characters in games featured on this website can range from almost completely human (people with cat ears) to almost completely non-human (cats who communicate or think using human language).

Purpose

This website was originally made to record all video games in which animals are important to the plot or gameplay, but I realized this topic was way too broad. This website will now record only Japanese or Japanese-inspired games, anime, and manga featuring kemono and kemonomimi characters.

Why?

Compared to western art styles, I prefer the Japanese anime art style. I feel like some people have acquired an aversion to animal characters because of the western furry community, so I wanted to expose people to the Japanese varient which has crossover with the more openly accepted anime and manga community. I also wanted to make it more easy to find and share games in this particular niche. A lot of times really interesting things go unnoticed because of the language barrier or because they don't rank well on popular search engines.
